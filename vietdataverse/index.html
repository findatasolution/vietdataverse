Gold Prices Surge to Record High of 80 Million VND per Tael

VnExpress Jan 5, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 14:30 H·ªìng Chi√™u

SJC gold bars traded in Hanoi today (Jan 5) increased by 500,000 VND compared to the previous session, reaching 79.8 - 80.2 million VND/tael (buy - sell), according to data from DOJI Gold, Silver and Gemstones Company.

This price represents a 0.6% increase compared to last weekend's trading session and marks the highest level in the past three months. According to experts, the primary driver stems from rising global gold prices as the USD weakens and concerns about global inflation intensify.

Meanwhile, Phu Quy silver bars also showed an upward trend, reaching 980,000 - 1,020,000 VND/tael, up approximately 20,000 VND from last week. Investors are actively seeking precious metals as a safe-haven investment channel amid volatile stock market conditions.

The VN-Index closed today at 1,235 points, down 1.2% from the previous session. Banking and real estate stocks faced the strongest selling pressure. However, gold-related stocks such as PNJ and SJC showed positive gains.

According to expert forecasts, gold prices may continue to rise next week if the U.S. Federal Reserve (Fed) maintains interest rates at current levels without signaling monetary policy tightening.

VN-Index Falls 1.2% Amid Banking Sector Pressure

CafeF Jan 5, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 12:15 Minh Tu·∫•n

The VN-Index closed at 1,235.4 points on January 5, down 15.2 points (-1.2%) from the previous session, marking the steepest single-day decline in nearly two weeks. Trading volume reached 850 million shares worth VND 18.5 trillion.

Banking stocks led the downturn, with VCB falling 2.1%, TCB down 1.8%, and MBB declining 1.5%. Analysts attribute the sell-off to profit-taking following recent gains and concerns over potential credit growth restrictions in 2026.

Real estate stocks also faced significant pressure. VHM dropped 2.3% while VIC shed 1.9%. However, the precious metals segment bucked the trend, with PNJ gaining 1.8% on the back of surging gold prices.

Foreign investors were net sellers for the third consecutive session, offloading VND 420 billion worth of Vietnamese stocks. Market experts recommend caution in the near term while monitoring corporate earnings reports due later this month.

State Bank Maintains Interbank Rate at 4.5%

VietStock Jan 4, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 16:45

The State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) announced it will maintain the overnight interbank interest rate at 4.5% for the foreseeable future, citing stable inflation and steady economic growth as key factors in the decision.

This marks the fourth consecutive month without a rate adjustment, signaling the central bank's confidence in the current monetary policy stance. The rate has remained unchanged since September 2025.

SBV Governor emphasized that Vietnam's inflation rate of 3.25% year-on-year remains well within the government's target of 4-4.5% for 2026. GDP growth is projected at 6.5-7% this year, supporting the case for policy stability.
